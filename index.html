Bio

Currently I am working as a Humboldt Experienced Researcher at Ruhr University Bochum, Germany. I am also serving as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Physics, Manipal University Jaipur, India. Prior to this, I was a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellow (2023–2025) at the Institute of Advanced Materials, Sweden, funded by the European Commission, and a Prof. Kobayashi Shunsuke Postdoctoral Fellow at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden (2023). I also served as Group Leader at the VBRI Innovation Center, New Delhi (2022–2023). My research interests include chalcogenide-based materials for phase-change memory, perovskite and kesterite photovoltaics for energy harvesting, self-powered piezoelectric/triboelectric/pyroelectric nanogenerators, surface plasmon resonance sensors, and advanced 2D nanomaterials for energy and sensing applications. I received International Association of Advanced Materials Europe Scientist Award (2024), and the Marie Skłodowska-Curie Individual Fellowship (2023).

I received the B.Sc. degree in Physics, Electronics and Mathematics from D.D.U. Gorakhpur University, India, in 2010; the M.Sc. degree in Electronics and Communication from Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya, Indore, India, in 2012; the M.Tech. degree in Optoelectronics and Laser Technology from Pt. Ravishankar Shukla University, Raipur, India, in 2015; and the Ph.D. degree in Physics from Madan Mohan Malaviya University of Technology, Gorakhpur, India, in 2019.
